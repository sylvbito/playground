## Orbit proof-of-practice workbench

### Design stance
A public, spatial workbench that makes Sylvain’s design-engineering judgement inspectable without pretending confidential work can be fully published.

### What it demonstrates
- **Work is the hero:** Orbit is a live public experiment rather than a decorative OS shell.
- **Private work stays honest:** field notes describe problems and decisions without leaking client systems.
- **AI is navigation:** `⌘/Ctrl + K` searches grounded evidence; it does not simulate a generic chatbot.

### Controls
- Drag panels by their small top handle on desktop.
- Use the rail to switch the framing state.
- Press `⌘/Ctrl + K`, click **Ask the workbench**, or use the evidence card to open the command palette.
- Try queries such as `CMS`, `systems`, `AI`, `Orbit`, or `internal work`.

### Trade-off
It deliberately avoids the fake desktop/dock metaphor. The spatial canvas borrows Orbit’s interaction language but the content remains a portfolio workbench, not a nostalgic operating-system imitation.
